quantitative & computational ecology

We use thinking-boxes and a lot of numbers to learn about where animals and green things live, and what they eat.

Our research on how complex ecological systems function, change, and evolve, relies on simulations, mathematics, and machine learning. We have a specific interest in ecological networks, biogeography, and epidemics. We believe in open science, in ecological synthesis based on open data, and in actionable fundamental research.

We are located in the Campus MIL of Université de Montréal.
We are mainly funded by NSERC, FRQNT, CFI, IVADO, and Wellcome.
